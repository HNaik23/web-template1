Business contracts form the foundation of commercial relationships, defining rights, obligations, and expectations between parties. While contracts aim to prevent disputes, conflicts can still arise, potentially leading to costly and time-consuming litigation. Arbitration, a form of Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR), is an effective way to resolve business disputes while safeguarding interests. This article explores the role of arbitration in business contracts, its benefits, and key considerations for ensuring protection in contractual agreements.

Disputes are common due to contract breaches, project delays, design defects, and payment disagreements. Traditional litigation can be costly, time-consuming, and disruptive to ongoing projects. Arbitration, a form of Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR), offers an efficient and cost-effective way to resolve construction disputes while keeping projects on track. This article explores the role of arbitration in construction conflicts and why it is a preferred method for dispute resolution.
